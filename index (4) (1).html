How News Channels Portray False News as True — Detection Guide
News channels often present misinformation as verified fact using emotional language, selective framing, and authority signals. This module explains exactly how they do it — and gives you the tools to detect and identify it yourself, both manually and using MisinfoRadar's AI pipeline.

🎭
Emotional Language
Designed to make you feel, not think
  • FEAR WORDS: "DEADLY," "CRISIS," "DANGER," "THREAT," "PANIC"
  • ANGER TRIGGERS: "OUTRAGE," "DISGRACEFUL," "BETRAYAL," "CORRUPT"
  • HOW TO DETECT: Real journalism is measured. Count emotional adjectives — more than 3 per paragraph is a red flag.
  • MisinfoRadar flags dominant emotions like fear and anger as high virality amplifiers (weight: 0.90, 0.85).
▼
📰
Sensational Headlines
Headline contradicts or exaggerates the article
  • Headline says "MILLIONS DEAD" — article says "a few hundred cases"
  • CLICKBAIT PATTERN: "You won't BELIEVE what happened…"
  • HOW TO DETECT: Always read past the headline. Does the body actually prove the claim?
  • Indian news tickers (crawl text) are especially notorious for this tactic.
▼
🔗
Missing Sources
"Sources say" — but which sources?
  • Vague: "experts believe," "many people say," "sources claim"
  • ASK: Which expert? From which institution? Published where?
  • HOW TO DETECT: Real news names specific people, institutions, or documents.
  • MisinfoRadar pattern: "many people are saying" scores +0.10 misinfo weight.
▼
🗺️
No Cross-Verification
Only one channel is running this "exclusive"
  • RULE: If a major story exists only on one outlet — be very suspicious.
  • Check BBC, Reuters, AP, PTI — do they carry it independently?
  • India: Cross-check with pib.gov.in/factcheck for government-related claims.
  • HOW TO DETECT: Search the headline in quotes on Google News.
▼
📸
Out-of-Context Media
Real images used to illustrate false stories
  • Old photos or videos presented as "live" or "breaking"
  • Images from another country used for local events
  • TOOL: Google Reverse Image Search → images.google.com
  • TOOL: InVID / WeVerify browser extension — checks video metadata and upload date.
▼
⏱️
Urgency & Share Pressure
"Share before they delete this!"
  • Creates artificial pressure to act before verifying
  • PHRASES: "SHARE IMMEDIATELY," "BREAKING," "MUST READ," "BANNED INFORMATION"
  • RULE: The more urgently something asks you to share, the more slowly you should respond.
  • MisinfoRadar flags "share immediately" at +0.15 misinfo weight.
▼
🧩
Conspiracy Framing
A secret group is hiding the truth from you
  • "The government doesn't want you to know this"
  • "Mainstream media won't tell you" / "Hidden truth"
  • "Big Pharma is hiding the cure" / "Deep State"
  • WHY IT'S DANGEROUS: Any counter-evidence becomes "part of the cover-up."
  • MisinfoRadar: conspiracy patterns score +0.20 to +0.30 misinfo weight.
▼
🎯
Selective Omission
True facts arranged to create a false impression
  • Every fact stated may be technically true — but crucial context is omitted.
  • Example: "Crime rose 40% under Party X" — omitting it rose 40% nationally too.
  • HOW TO DETECT: Ask "what is this story NOT telling me?"
  • Find the full dataset, not just the quoted statistic.
▼
🤖
Deepfakes & AI Content
AI-generated video/audio of real people saying false things
  • AI video of a politician saying something they never said
  • SIGNS: Unnatural blinking, face boundary blur, voice cadence issues
  • TOOL: Deepware Scanner → deepware.ai
  • TOOL: Microsoft Video Authenticator for frame-by-frame deepfake analysis

Real News vs. Fake News — Side by Side
✅ Real News
📌Named sources with specific credentials (Dr. Meena Sharma, AIIMS cardiologist)
📌Balanced — includes counter-arguments and opposing expert views
📌Has a corrections policy — publicly corrects errors
📌Measured language — no ALL CAPS, no excessive exclamation marks
📌Links to original documents, studies, or official statements
📌Opinion clearly separated from factual reporting
📌Covered independently by multiple reputable outlets
❌ Fake News
⚠️Vague attribution — "sources say," "experts believe," "many are saying"
⚠️One-sided — no attempt to include counter-facts or dissenting experts
⚠️Never issues corrections — deletes posts instead
⚠️Extreme emotional language — fear, rage, shock throughout every paragraph
⚠️No links to primary sources — only links to other opinion articles
⚠️Opinion and "news" are intermingled with no distinction
⚠️Only found on one or two fringe outlets — no mainstream pickup
